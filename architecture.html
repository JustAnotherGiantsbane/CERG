CERG · Cybersecurity Operating Model

Document architecture · Three pillars · 119 artifacts · CC BY 4.0

Three pillars, one team

  • Engineering builds security in early — architecture review, standards, configuration baselines
  • Risk knows exposure — exposure management, adversarial testing, threat intelligence
  • Governance enables the business — policy, compliance, evidence, reporting
  • Cross-pillar flows (FLOW-001) connect all three through 7 operational workflows

Document types

  • Spine (8) — the minimum viable CERG. Policy, Framework, OM, Catalog, RMF, Risk Register + templates, VM
  • Standards (15) — mandatory requirements by technology domain
  • Procedures (12) — how to execute processes
  • Plans & Templates (17) — operational packages and fill-in artifacts
  • Workforce (35) — job families, competency model, per-role descriptions

Key ideas

  • Yes-and default — enable the business with guardrails; a slow yes is a no
  • Implementation ≠ effectiveness — CEF-001 measures whether controls work, not just whether they exist
  • Bottom-up + top-down — the finding pipeline (bottom-up) is pressure-tested against crown-jewel scenarios (top-down)
  • Reciprocal commitments — SLC-001 holds CERG's own responsiveness to the same standard it holds the business
